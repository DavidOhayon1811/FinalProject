This is my Final Project of 10th Grade, I'm very happy about it. I learnt tons about web development while I was making this project like:

  • Usage of an Api, getting information (data) from another site and displaying it on my own for example I used an Api for displaying all my photos on the image gallery.
  • Local Storage, how to store what the user's done or chosen and maintain it on refresh and saved across browser sessions. It's very useful when you need to save the user's preferences on your website.
  • Css Flexbox, The main idea behind the flex layout is to give the container the ability to alter its itemsâ€™ width/height to best fill the available space mostly to accommodate to all kind of display devices and screen sizes.
  • Media queries, they're useful when you want to modify your site or depending on the user's device or specific characteristics and parameters such as screen resolution or browser viewport width.

Now that you know what I learned, I'm going to give you a tour of this project.

  1. First you should check out the about page to learn a little bit about me and my life, you can also contact me there if you'd like.
  2. Next there's the immage gallery, I talked a bit about it before. The photos comes from pexels.com. You can look at the "curated photos" (some random photos given by pexels) when you load the page, you also have the possibility to search for photos.

Now that you've seen and explored the surroundings of my project, let's get to the main part: the projects, you can access them by clicking on the dropdown button.

  1. First Project is "The BeatMaker". This one is pretty fun, all you've got to do is click on the Kick, Snare and Hihats boxes and press play to make awesome beats. You have a variety of Kick, Snare and Hihats classic to electro that you can try out, you can alse change the tempo, lower to chill and higher to rock. To create the perfect beat, then you can mute each track with the mute button to perfectionate your beat.
  2. The second one called "ToDo List" is rather basic but very useful. If you ever forget your timetable, then you should use the ToDo List. You can write whatever you need to do throughout your day. and check it whenever you're finished with the task or even delete it to completely forget about it. If you ever closed your ToDo List tab, you can just open it back and every one of the tasks that you've added to your list will be there. (there's a small bug though, if you've checked one of the task and refresh the page then the task will be there but unchecked)
  3. The last one is "The Coloors" project and was strongly inspired from the site coolors.co but I literally just replaced the place of the double "o". With this project you can generate creative palettes of colors, save them with their respective name, open them in your library and even edit them. To create the perfect palette you have multiple ways. You can generate colors, lock one you like and repeat the process until you have created the perfect palette. You can also edit the hue, brightness and saturation of each color to find the perfect color for your palette. and by clicking on the hexadecimal color text the hex code will be automatically copied to your clipboard.
